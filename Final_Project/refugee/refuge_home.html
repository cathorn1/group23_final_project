Refugee Information Hub

About us:

This project is inspired by the increasing number of refugees applying for refugee resettlement to the United States. One of the members of our group, originally from Daraa, Syria, received refugee designation by the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) during the Syrian Civil War and was approved by the U.S. State Department to be resettled in the United States. In 2016, he was then resettled by one of the nine national refugee resettlement agencies in the United States, HIAS, whose local affiliate, Carolina Refugee Resettlement Agency, resettled him in Charlotte. His experience has motivated us to create a product to assist future refugees, policymakers, resettlement agencies, and researchers.


Product Vision:

FOR policymakers, resettlement agencies, researchers, and refugees who need access to data regarding refugee resettlement in the United States, WHO would benefit from an information dashboard representing the state of refugee resettlement in the U.S. THE Refugee Information Hub is a dashboard website THAT explores the people and locations impacted by the relocation of refugees in the U.S.. UNLIKE the UN dataset, OUR PRODUCT streamlines pertinent information in one convenient location, is focused on the U.S., North Carolina and Charlotte, and is easy to use by anyone, and will provide access to various sources.

Refugee Information Hub was designed/created by Group 23 of the Software Engineering class at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte.
